Chinese Exams – YCT (Youth Chinese Test)

 The Chinese Proficiency Test（(Youth)/YCT, an international standardized test for Chinese language proficiency, assesses primary and secondary school students' abilities to use Chinese as a second language in their daily and academic life.

 The writing test consists of four levels.
The speaking test consists of two levels, including primary and
intermediate.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Is Chinese too difficult for children?

A: No. Children often learn faster than adults due to brain plasticity, natural mimicry abilities, and fewer inhibitions about making mistakes.

2. What age should my child start learning Chinese?

Children can start learning Chinese as early as 2-3 years old. The earlier the better for pronunciation and natural acquisition, with ages 3-7 being optimal for developing native-like fluency

3. How long does it take kids to learn Mandarin?

Basic conversational skills: 1-2 years with regular practice. Fluency: 5-7 years.Academic proficiency: 7-10 years.
Timeline varies by exposure frequency and individual factors.

4. Is it possible to learn Chinese online for my kid?

Yes. We offer online courses where kids learn Chinese with native speakers. Teachers use interactive games and engaging activities to make learning fun and effective. Combine with offline practice for best results.

5.How much practice time is needed daily?

15-30 minutes daily for young children, up to 1 hour for older kids. Consistency matters more than duration.

6. What learning materials do you use for children?

We select the best domestic and international textbooks tailored to each child's age and proficiency level, including materials from Beijing Language and Culture University (BLCU), China's leading institution for teaching Chinese as a foreign language.
